Common Questions

FAQ

How should I use Epigram effectively?
Follow the 4-week structure in order. Spend 1–2 hours per day: read the concept box, attempt the free problems, then check the solutions. Don't move to the next topic until you can explain the key insight out loud. Consistency over cramming — the daily problem keeps you sharp between sessions.
What are the prerequisites?
You should be comfortable with: undergraduate probability (random variables, distributions, expectation), basic statistics (hypothesis testing, regression), and introductory algorithms (time complexity, recursion, basic data structures). You do not need prior finance knowledge — Epigram covers the options and game theory content from first principles.
What if I'm still confused after reading a solution?
Email us directly — all Premium users get unlimited email support. For deeper help, we offer 1-on-1 tutoring sessions with the author at $120/hr, including a free 15-minute discovery call. No question is too basic.
Is Epigram only for quant finance roles?
Primarily yes — the curriculum is optimised for quant trader, quant researcher, and quant developer roles at top firms. That said, the probability, statistics, and coding modules are directly applicable to data science and ML engineering interviews. Game theory and options pricing are less relevant for non-finance roles.
How is this different from LeetCode or the Green Book?
LeetCode is designed for software engineering interviews — most of its problems are irrelevant to quant interviews. The Green Book (2008) and Heard on the Street were written before modern quant interviews evolved. Epigram is the only platform combining a structured 4-week curriculum with real, current firm problems and human-verified solutions.
How often are new problems added?
Continuously. Problems are added as the author collects them from recent interview reports, direct firm contacts, and user submissions. Premium users get priority access to new problems as they're added. You can also submit a problem you encountered. Accepted problems are publicly credited to you on the platform.
